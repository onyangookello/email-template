Areas of Improvement

1. Insecure Site

Currently when customers visit your website they are greeted with the dreaded red padlock warning: "Connection is not secure". You will need to secure your site by purchasing an SSL certificate.

2. Confusing Brand Identity

No coherence in the choice of colors, fonts, graphics, logos, images, etc. creates the impression of a "kienyeji" brand and may lead to customers not trusting your brand. However, this can be fixed by choosing brand colors, fonts, and style and sticking to that.

3. Broken Components

Links that don't work. Buttons whose styling is jumbled. Animations that break. Spelling mistakes. I could go on. This just screams to customers that the brand does not care about how it present itself to the world. More testing before launch will help reveal such mistakes and have them fixed beforehand.

4. Confusing Message

Most importantly: what story are you telling? What should customers do once they are on your website? What are the KPIs for your website? Is it inquiries, quotations, just to redirect customers to your shop. That message is not clearly communicated in the current website. A redesign would need to have a consistent message.

5. Overall, a Bland Visual Appeal

The overall effect is that the customer is overwhelmed with many colors, a broken structure, mixed fonts, bad visual design and is unlikely to trust Pabari Enterprises Limited as a professional business. If this is how their website looks like, then maybe that also translates to their customer service. Customers are therefore unlikely to reach out from the website.
